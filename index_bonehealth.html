Health Guidelines

Essential Patient's Bone and Joint Health Information
üåü

Essential Bone and Joint Health Practices

  • Regular orthopedic health check-ups, especially after age 50 or menopause
  • Maintain a calcium- and vitamin D-rich diet (dairy, leafy greens, fortified foods, eggs, fish)
  • Engage in weight-bearing activities (walking, dancing, stair climbing) for 30+ minutes daily
  • Add twice-weekly resistance and balance exercises (strength training, yoga, tai chi)
  • Maintain a healthy body weight; reduce excess load on joints
  • Quit smoking and avoid excess alcohol to protect bone density
  • Aim for 7-9 hours of quality sleep for bone and tissue repair
  • Stay hydrated for optimal joint lubrication
üíâ

Essential Vaccinations for Bone, Muscle, and Joint Health

  • Tetanus vaccine: For injury and post-surgery protection
  • Influenza (flu) vaccine: Annually for chronic bone/joint conditions and elderly
  • COVID-19 vaccine: Strongly recommended for musculoskeletal and immune-compromised patients
  • Pneumococcal vaccine (PCV13/PPSV23): Prevents pneumonia and joint/bone infections in at-risk adults
  • Haemophilus influenzae type b (Hib) vaccine: Childhood protection against bone/joint infections
  • Measles-Mumps-Rubella (MMR) vaccine: Reduces risk of arthritis and joint complications
  • Polio vaccine: Prevents muscle weakness and deformity (childhood and risk settings)
  • Varicella (chickenpox) vaccine: Prevents rare bone/joint complications of chickenpox
  • BCG (Tuberculosis) vaccine: Prevents bone/joint TB in endemic regions
  • Shingles (herpes zoster) vaccine: For adults >50 to prevent nerve/muscle pain
  • Hepatitis B vaccine: Important for those undergoing joint or bone surgery
üî¨

Bone & Joint Screening Guidelines

  • Osteoporosis: DEXA scan for women after menopause and men >70 years or with risk factors
  • Vitamin D Level: Regular screening in older adults, those with fractures, or limited sunlight exposure
  • Calcium Level: Check if osteoporosis, malabsorption, or chronic steroid use
  • Bone Age X-ray: In children with growth disorders or suspected bone diseases
  • Spinal Health: Scoliosis screening in adolescents and postmenopausal women
  • Knee/Hip X-ray: If persistent pain, swelling, or instability in major joints
  • Fall Risk Assessment: Annual review for those >65 years, those with prior falls or gait problems
  • Rheumatoid Factor/ESR/CRP: If persistent joint swelling, pain, or morning stiffness
  • Bone Tumor Risk: Imaging or biopsy if suspicious, painful, or growing bone lumps
  • BMD Baseline Screening: Early screening if strong family history or previous fragility fracture
üõ°Ô∏è

Preventive Measures for Bone & Joint Health

  • Get recommended childhood and adult vaccinations to prevent bone/joint infections (measles, influenza, Hib, meningococcal, pneumococcal).
  • Practice safe sports: use protective gear, warm up, and cross-train to avoid overuse injuries.
  • Strengthen home safety for fall prevention: install grab bars, use non-slip rugs, and keep walkways clear.
  • Avoid long-term use of steroids and certain acid-reducing/antiepileptic drugs unless medically necessary.
  • Manage chronic illnesses (diabetes, thyroid, kidney disease) that can raise bone/joint risk.
  • Seek prompt treatment for any persistent joint pain, swelling, night pain, or unexplained deformity.
  • Ensure ergonomic workstations and take regular movement breaks to prevent postural/joint injuries.
  • Monitor for side effects of medications affecting bone health, especially if postmenopausal or elderly.
  • For children: encourage outdoor play, limit screen/device time, and treat sports injuries early.
  • For older adults: vision and hearing checks, review medications, and maintain social connections to reduce fall risk.
  • Undergo genetic counseling or early screening/testing if there is a family history of brittle bone disease, repeated fractures, or metabolic bone disorder.
  • Wear appropriate footwear for every activity to protect feet, ankles, knees, and spine.
  • Balance calcium supplementation with dietary intake and consult a doctor before starting supplements if you have cardiac, kidney, or thyroid issues.
  • Protect from workplace bone hazards: use safe lifting techniques and required personal protective equipment (PPE).
