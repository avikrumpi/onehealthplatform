Essential Health Practices

  • BCG Vaccine: At birth‚Äîprotects against severe forms of tuberculosis
  • Hepatitis B Vaccine: First dose at birth, with additional doses at 6, 10, and 14 weeks
  • Oral Polio Vaccine (OPV): Birth dose, then at 6, 10, 14 weeks, and booster at 16‚Äì24 months
  • DTP (Diphtheria, Tetanus, Pertussis): At 6, 10, and 14 weeks; boosters at 16‚Äì24 months and 4‚Äì6 years
  • Haemophilus Influenzae type B (Hib): At 6, 10, 14 weeks, and booster at 16‚Äì24 months
  • Rotavirus Vaccine: First dose at 6 weeks, second and third doses at 10 and 14 weeks
  • Pneumococcal Conjugate Vaccine (PCV): At 6, 10, 14 weeks; booster at 12‚Äì15 months
  • Measles and Rubella (MR/MMR): First dose at 9‚Äì12 months and second dose at 16‚Äì24 months; further boosters may be given at 4‚Äì6 years
  • Varicella (Chickenpox) Vaccine: At 12‚Äì18 months (some guidelines recommend a booster at 4‚Äì6 years)
  • Hepatitis A Vaccine: First dose at 12 months, second dose 6‚Äì12 months later
  • Typhoid Vaccine: Single dose at age 2 years and boosters every 3 years as needed
  • Influenza Vaccine: Annually starting at 6 months (two doses the first year)
  • Japanese Encephalitis Vaccine: At 9 and 16 months old (in endemic regions)
  • HPV Vaccine (Human Papillomavirus): Two-dose series begins at age 9‚Äì12 years, with completion by age 14
  • Tdap (Tetanus, Diphtheria, Acellular Pertussis): At 10‚Äì12 years and every 10 years thereafter
  • Meningococcal Vaccine: First dose at age 11‚Äì12 years and booster at 16 years
  • Regular health screenings and check-ups: Annual pediatrician visits for growth, development, and health assessments
  • Vision screening: At birth, before school entry, and as recommended by pediatrician
  • Hearing screening: At birth and as needed throughout childhood
  • Dental check-ups: Every 6 months starting at age 1 year
üî¨

Screening Guidelines

  • Vision Health: Regular eye screening at birth, during infancy, preschool, and school-age to detect vision problems early
  • Hearing Health: Hearing tests at birth, before school entry, and during childhood to catch hearing loss promptly
  • Growth & BMI: Height, weight, and BMI measurements during each pediatric check-up to monitor for obesity or growth problems
  • Blood Pressure: Annual blood pressure checks for children over age 3 to detect hypertension or heart/kidney risks
  • Dental Health: Dental exams and cleanings every 6 months to prevent cavities and gum disease
  • Anemia Screen: emoglobin and hematocrit tests at key ages or for children at risk of iron deficiency
  • Lead Poisoning: Lead blood tests between ages 1‚Äì2 or in high-risk environments to prevent developmental disorders
  • Cholesterol Screening: Universal lipid profile once between ages 9‚Äì11 and again 17‚Äì21 years (or earlier for high-risk children)
  • Diabetes Monitoring: Blood sugar checks for children with obesity, family history, or signs of insulin resistance
  • Immunization Status: Annual review of vaccines with catch-up on missed doses to prevent infectious diseases
üõ°Ô∏è

Preventive Measures

  • Complete all childhood vaccines according to schedule to prevent infectious diseases
  • Wash hands frequently to reduce risk of viral and bacterial illnesses
  • Encourage at least 60 minutes of active play or sports daily
  • Eat fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins every day
  • Brush teeth twice daily with fluoride toothpaste and schedule dental check-ups
  • Maintain adequate sleep : 9‚Äì12 hours for children (ages 6‚Äì12) and 8‚Äì10 hours for teens
  • Hydration:Drink water often throughout the day, especially during activities and hot weather
  • Healthy Weight Maintenance: Monitor BMI and focus on healthy diet and activity to prevent obesity
  • Mental Health Support : Encourage open conversations and seek help for stress, sadness, or anxiety
  • Avoid Smoking & Secondhand Smoke:Teach children to avoid tobacco exposure completely
  • Saftey & Awareness: Teach rules for personal safety, emergency contacts, and how to get help
  • Begin HPV vaccine starting at age 9‚Äì12 to prevent later cancers

Adolescent Health Programme (RKSK)

Focused services for 10‚Äì19-year-olds including nutrition, menstrual hygiene, mental health, sexual and reproductive health awareness

Operation Smile

Free corrective surgeries for children with cleft lip and palate, coordinated with public hospitals across West Bengal

Mobile Medical Units (MMU)/Child Health Camps

Outreach vans and periodic camps in rural and remote areas providing basic healthcare, immunization, and screening for children

Rashtriya Kishor Swasthya Karyakram (RKSK) Menstrual Hygiene Scheme

Balika Samriddhi Yojana

Promotes education and health of girl children through financial incentives and scholarships for school-going girls

School Health Programme (SHP)

Annual health screenings, immunizations, and health education sessions for school children covering nutrition, hygiene, and vaccination awareness

Safe Motherhood & Child Survival Programme

Antenatal, postnatal care, and institutional delivery promotion aiming to reduce maternal and child mortality and morbidity

ICDS Supplementary Nutrition Programme

Swachh Bharat Mission (School Toilets & Hygiene)

Mass immunization campaign to eliminate measles and rubella among children under 15 years

West Bengal initiative focusing on development & education of children below 6 through better early childhood learning environments
