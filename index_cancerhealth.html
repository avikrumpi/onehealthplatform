Essential Cancer Prevention & Healthy Living Practices

  • Schedule age-appropriate cancer screenings (breast, cervical, colon, oral, prostate) regularly
  • Eat a high-fiber, antioxidant-rich diet (fruits, vegetables, whole grains, pulses)
  • Limit processed, charred, and red meat products
  • Exercise at least 150 minutes per week: brisk walking, swimming, cycling, yoga
  • Maintain a healthy body weight and waist circumference
  • Quit smoking; avoid tobacco, betel nut, and excessive alcohol
  • Protect skin with sunscreen, hats, and coverage outdoors
  • Prioritize sleep and stress management‚Äîpractice mindfulness and seek social support
  • Use safe practices in sexual health and avoid risky behaviors
  • Stay hydrated and promote healthy digestion
üíâ

Essential Cancer-Related Vaccinations

  • HPV vaccine: Protects against cervical, anal, oral, and other HPV-related cancers
  • Hepatitis B vaccine: Prevents hepatitis B infection‚Äîa major liver cancer cause
  • Hepatitis A vaccine: For people at risk for chronic liver disease and secondary liver cancer
  • Influenza (flu) vaccine: Reduces infection risk in immunocompromised/cancer patients
  • Pneumococcal vaccine: For patients with weakened immunity during or after cancer treatment
  • COVID-19 vaccine: Strongly advised for all cancer patients and survivors
  • Varicella & shingles (herpes zoster) vaccine: Prevents viral complications in immunosuppressed patients
  • Haemophilus influenzae type b (Hib) vaccine: For stem cell transplant and high-risk cancer populations
üî¨

Cancer Risk & Screening Guidelines

  • Breast Cancer: Annual mammogram screening after age 40, or earlier with family history
  • Cervical Cancer: Pap smear every 3 years for women 21‚Äì65, plus HPV testing
  • Colon Cancer: Colonoscopy every 10 years after age 50, or earlier for high-risk patients
  • Oral Cancer: Annual oral cavity and throat exam if tobacco/alcohol user
  • Prostate Cancer: PSA blood test and prostate exam after age 50, earlier if family risk
  • Skin Cancer: Dermatologist full-body exam for high risk or with suspicious moles
  • Liver Cancer: Ultrasound and AFP blood test for hepatitis or cirrhosis, as advised
  • Lung Cancer: Low-dose CT scan for smokers or chronic lung disease patients, as recommended
  • Genetic Counseling: For those with strong family history (BRCA, Lynch syndrome, etc.)
  • Immunodeficiency Risk: Infection prevention and screening for cancer patients on chemotherapy
üõ°Ô∏è

Healthy Preventive Measures for Cancer Survivors

  • Continue regular medical follow-up and early symptom reporting for relapse screening
  • Engage in physical activity during and after cancer treatment (as advised)
  • Eat a balanced, nutrient-rich diet to support immune recovery
  • Avoid risky health behaviors (smoking, tobacco, excessive alcohol)
  • Practice safe sun exposure and self-exams for skin changes
  • Maintain strong social and family support‚Äîparticipate in survivor communities
  • Monitor for secondary cancers or treatment-related complications
  • Get regular vaccinations, especially flu, pneumococcal, HPV, COVID-19
  • Access mental health counseling to support recovery
  • Participate in screening programs if at hereditary risk
  • Inform healthcare providers about any long-term symptoms or medication effects
  • Engage with local support groups and educational programs

üéóÔ∏è Major Cancer Care Initiatives (Govt. of India)

Ayushman Bharat - PMJAY

Health cover up to ‚Çπ5 lakh per family/year for secondary & tertiary cancer care at empanelled hospitals

National Programme for Prevention and Control of Cancer, Diabetes, Cardiovascular Diseases and Stroke (NPCDCS)

Population-based screening for common cancers (oral, breast, cervical); strengthening cancer care infrastructure

National Cancer Registry Programme (NCRP)

Systematic cancer data collection for research, policy, and resource allocation

Pradhan Mantri Swasthya Suraksha Yojana (PMSSY)

Establishing AIIMS-like institutes and upgrading existing government medical colleges with cancer, oncology care facilities

Screening & Early Detection under NHM

Free routine screening for breast, cervical, and oral cancer at public health centers

INDIA Cancer Societies Support (NGO Collaboration)

Partnership for patient support, financial help, and awareness ‚Äì e.g. Indian Cancer Society, CanSupport, and more

National AYUSH Mission (NAM)

Integrating traditional medicine (Ayurveda, Yoga) for cancer palliative care and wellness

Rashtriya Arogya Nidhi (RAN)

Financial assistance for poor patients undergoing cancer treatment in government hospitals

VAN Cancer Care Programme (States)

State-run mobile cancer screening & early detection units, especially in rural areas

National Tobacco Control Programme (NTCP)

Cancer prevention through tobacco cessation campaigns, school education, and enforcement
