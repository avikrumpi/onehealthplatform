Essential Heart & Cardiovascular Health Practices

  • Schedule regular blood pressure and cholesterol checks (at least once a year after age 40 or at-risk)
  • Follow a heart-healthy diet high in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, nuts, seeds, and lean proteins
  • Limit salt, saturated fat, trans fat, and processed foods
  • Exercise at least 30 minutes a day, 5 days a week (brisk walking, cycling, swimming, dancing)
  • Maintain a healthy weight and waist circumference
  • Quit smoking and avoid all tobacco products
  • Limit or avoid alcohol consumption
  • Stay hydrated and manage stress through mindfulness, yoga, or social support
  • Prioritize 7-8 hours of restful sleep each night
  • Monitor blood sugar, especially if diabetic or pre-diabetic
üíâ

Essential Vaccinations for Heart & Vascular Health

  • Influenza (flu) vaccine: Reduces risk of heart attack and complications in heart disease patients
  • COVID-19 vaccine: Critical for people with heart disease or risk factors
  • Pneumococcal vaccine (PCV13/PPSV23): Prevents pneumonia and can reduce cardiac complications in vulnerable adults
  • Shingles (herpes zoster) vaccine: Reduces risk of stroke and complications in people over 50
  • Tetanus‚Äìdiphtheria‚Äìpertussis (Tdap) vaccine: Important for adults; prevents disease that may strain the heart
  • Hepatitis B vaccine: For people with heart failure, diabetes, or those on long-term care
üî¨

Heart & Cardiovascular Screening Guidelines

  • Blood Pressure: Annual screening for all adults, more frequently if high or at-risk
  • Lipid Profile: Check cholesterol every 4-6 years (more often if high/at-risk)
  • Blood Sugar/Diabetes: Fasting or HbA1c testing as per risk/medical advice
  • ECG (Electrocardiogram): Baseline after age 40 or with any heart risk factors/symptoms
  • Stress Test: If chest pain, symptoms, or high-risk profile
  • Echo/Ultrasound: If abnormal sounds, high BP, or known heart disease
  • Ankle-Brachial Index: Screen for peripheral artery disease in smokers, diabetics, elderly
  • Carotid Doppler: For stroke risk assessment in selected patients
  • Coronary Calcium Scan: May help in selected patients for risk estimation (doctor-guided)
  • Body Mass Index (BMI): Calculate yearly or when there is weight change
üõ°Ô∏è

Preventive Measures for Heart & Vascular Health

  • Take prescribed medications for blood pressure, sugar, and cholesterol as directed
  • Manage chronic illnesses (hypertension, diabetes, kidney disease) closely
  • Attend regular doctor follow-ups and heart check programs
  • Respond promptly to chest pain, unexplained breathlessness, or fainting ‚Äì call emergency services if needed
  • Practice stress management (deep breathing, meditation, hobbies, professional support)
  • Eat a heart-healthy diet and avoid excess processed or fatty foods
  • Keep an emergency contact and medicine list handy
  • Use safe practices to prevent infections (handwashing, dental health, avoid sick contacts)
  • Get vaccinated as per guidelines, especially influenza and pneumococcal vaccines
  • Engage in social and family support programs for better recovery and healthy lifestyle
